# OS-PM Advisory Committee — Charter v0.1

**Status:** Draft for v1 ratification at first committee meeting.
**Effective:** Upon first majority sign-off (target: Phase 2 kickoff).
**Authors:** [PI name], on behalf of the OS-PM project.

---

## 1. Purpose

The OS-PM Advisory Committee provides independent academic, technical,
and policy oversight of the OS-PM project. The committee:

1. Reviews scope-policy compliance, especially Volume 11 exclusion.
2. Approves or vetoes proposed schema additions.
3. Vets historical-case selections and validation methodology.
4. Endorses (or declines to endorse) public releases — code, preprints,
   datasets.
5. Provides domain expertise in safeguards, satellite imagery, machine
   learning, and nonproliferation policy.

The committee operates under the OS-PM scope policy: detection-only
academic research; no Volume 11 weaponization content; no operational
sanctions or strike content.

## 2. Composition

Five seats:

| Seat | Profile | Indicative candidates (public information only) |
|------|---------|--------------------------------------------------|
| **Chair** | Senior, ~30 years safeguards experience; ex-IAEA preferred | (KAERI senior; KINAC senior; ex-IAEA inspector) |
| **Technical (AI / VLM)** | Active research in vision-language models | (KAIST 전산학부; NAVER AI Lab; SNU 컴퓨터공학부) |
| **Technical (GIS / RS)** | Satellite imagery analysis specialist | (서울대 지구환경공학부; KARI; KIST) |
| **Policy** | Nonproliferation policy / international relations | (KAIST 핵·양자공학과; 국방연구원; 통일연구원) |
| **Ethics** | Research ethics / dual-use research of concern | (Institutional IRB officer; bioethics academic) |

**Diversity targets:**
- At least 1 member from outside the PI's institution
- At least 1 member with non-Korean affiliation (post-Phase 1)
- At least 1 woman and 1 person under 50 years of age

## 3. Term and renewal

- Initial term: **2 years**, renewable once.
- Members may resign with 30-day notice.
- Chair can be replaced by majority vote of remaining members.

## 4. Compensation

- The committee is **uncompensated** (consistent with academic norms in
  KR/JP/EU).
- Travel reimbursement for in-person meetings (max 1/year), subject to
  project funding.
- Co-authorship offered for substantive contributions per ICMJE
  criteria, at the PI's discretion.

## 5. Conflicts of interest

Members disclose at appointment and annually:

- Equity / consulting in companies producing safeguards-relevant
  technology (Sandia, Orano, Westinghouse, etc.)
- Funding relationships with IAEA / KOSTI / NRF that could be perceived
  as conflicting.
- Co-authorship with active proliferation programmes (none expected, but
  disclosed).

A standing Recusal Procedure: members recuse from votes where direct or
material interests apply.

## 6. Meetings

- **Cadence:** Quarterly minimum; ad-hoc on PI request.
- **Quorum:** 3 of 5 members; chair must concur for any veto.
- **Minutes:** Recorded by PI, distributed within 7 days, archived in
  `docs/advisory/minutes/`.
- **Decisions:** Majority of present-and-voting members. Tied votes
  default to no-action.

## 7. Veto authority

The committee **may** (not must):

- Veto a proposed PM cell or schema change for scope-policy reasons.
- Block a public release pending revisions.
- Require redaction of specific content before any release.

The committee **may not**:

- Direct day-to-day project work.
- Override the PI's choice of co-authors.
- Compel publication or disclosure beyond legal/ethical minimum.

## 8. Confidentiality

Committee members access:

- Internal planning documents (`PI_candidates_open_source_research.md`).
- Pre-publication drafts of papers.
- Sensitive case-handling discussions.

Members agree to keep these confidential during the project term and
for two years after departure. The committee's existence and member
names are public; meeting minutes are partially redacted.

## 9. External communication

- The committee speaks **as a committee**, not as individuals, in any
  official communication.
- Individual members may publicly comment on OS-PM as researchers, with
  appropriate disclosure.
- Press inquiries route through the PI; the chair may decline.

## 10. Scope policy reaffirmation

Members affirm that:

- Volume 11 (weaponization) is permanently out of scope.
- Operational sanctions / strike planning is out of scope.
- Classified or distribution-restricted material is not used.
- Active / undisclosed proliferation cases are not analysed by OS-PM.

## 11. Amendment

This charter may be amended by:

- Unanimous committee vote, or
- PI proposal + majority committee vote.
